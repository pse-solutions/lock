Getting Started - Additional Lock Settings

1. How to Check Unlock Status

Steps to check unlock status

  1. Select the lock you want to view the unlock status
  2. Tap the icon on the top right corner
  3. The status of the lock is returned as "Locked or "Unlocked"

2. Configuring Auto Lock

All locks are equipped with auto lock functionality. However, only specific locks are designed to operate without auto lock.

Lock Model Auto Lock Off
IntelliBolt ✔
IntelliBolt 2 ✔
IntelliLever ✖
IntelliLever 2 ✖
IntelliMortise ✖

Steps to configure auto lock

  1. Select the lock you want to view the unlock status, then tap "Settings".
  1. Tap on "Auto Lock" and toggle Auto Lock On or Off. If toggled on, tap on "Delay Time" and specify the seconds that a lock will auto lock.

3. Passage Mode

Passage mode allows you to set schedules for specific locks to remain unlocked. When in unlocked state, lock users do not have to input any credentials to enter the room.

Use cases for passage mode include:

  • Keeping doors unlocked in specific common areas (pool access, etc.)
  • Keeping doors unlocked for staff, contractors, or maintenance personnel who may need access to rooms or areas throughout the day.

Steps to enable passage mode

  1. Select the lock you want to view the unlock status, then tap "Settings"
  1. Tap on "Passage Mode", and then tap the "Turn On" button.
  1. Tap the toggle on, and you will see the "Time Period" section appear.
  1. Tap the blue "+" button, and select the days and the timeframe for which you want the lock to remain open.

*If you do not input a schedule, the lock will remain open until the passage mode is toggled off.

*With the free version of TTLock, users can only add one schedule per lock.

  1. The final step to enable passage mode on a lock is to present any valid credential to the lock during the specified schedule. The lock will notify you that it's in passage mode.
