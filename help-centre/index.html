Frequently Asked Questions

How far should I install my TTLock gateways from my 33 Locks?

Gateways should generally be installed as close to locks as possible. The recommended maximum range of a gateway is 20-30 feet, but could be impacted by obstructions between the gateway and lock.

Additional TTLock gateways can always be added after installation.

What happens to my 33 Locks during an internet outage?

Your 33 Locks will continue to operate with all previously installed credentials (PINs, cards, etc.). Already installed credentials will become active and deactivate according to their defined schedule.

New credentials cannot be added or deleted remotely during the outage and must be added directly at the lock, or generated offline.

What happens to my 33 Locks if a gateway loses internet connectivity or power?

TTLock gateways create a many-to-many (M2M) mesh network, which means if any one gateway loses power or connectivity, your 33 Lock will attempt to connect to the next closest gateway.

How often do I need to update firmware on my locks?

Firmware updates are infrequent, and can occur approximately 2 times a year. While 33 Lock encourages all users to update firmware when available, updates are most often optional, and may not be required to install immediately. See Lock Firmware Instructions here.

What to do if my 33 Lock dies suddenly?

  1. Plug a power bank into the bottom of the lock to attempt to provide power.
    1. If this works, it's likely that the batteries are dead. Replace asap.
  2. If the lock still doesn't power up, use a metal key to access the unit.
  3. Remove the battery cover and inspect the battery terminals for corrosion or other obvious damage.
  4. If the terminals look okay, replace them with brand new batteries to see if this resolves the issue.
  5. If the lock still doesn't power up, remove the lock from the door and inspect the cable connecting the front and back of the lock.
    1. Confirm that the two ends are securely connected.
    2. Confirm there is no damage to the cable.
  6. If there are no signs of connection issues or damage, replace the lock with one from your attic stock.
    1. It's okay to keep the mortise chassis in the door and just replace the front and back.
  7. Add the new lock on to the TTLock app.
    1. If using an access automation partner, contact them asap to onboard the new lock.
  8. Contact your installer and support@33lock.com to create a warranty claim.
